Competition and Data Description

  • Competition description: AIHACKCOVID is the first hackathon event I participated in, only lasted 48 hours. The goal is to learn from the worldwide data on the pandemic so we can prevent or better manage future pandamics. Specifically, the sponsors are prefer to hospitalizations and policy decisions, so to advise Dutch policy makers.
  • Data description: We are only allowed to use the owid, which collected the covid general information in the whole world, and oxcgrt, which is about the goverment response to covid.
  • Difficulties:

    There is no clear task, no metrics. You have to find a research question by yourself, which is hard for people not related to that field. More importantly, how to find a good question will be crucial, which is also my biggest benefit from that competition.

Our solution

Because lack of related background and the time limitation, we proposed 4 not very innovative questions:

  1. Does government response affect daily new cases? –> correlation.
  2. Does new vaccinations affect the number of daily cases and daily deaths? –> correlation and linear regression model explanation.
  3. Is it possible to use machine learning models for regression and report the importance of variables? –>regression models.
  4. Can a model be used to forecast the convergence of COVID-19 in the future? –> Sigmoid fitting.

Other solutions

Masks effective

Cluster same policy countries -> add “time shifted” variables(10 days) -> add “schools closing, working from home, or gathering restrictions” -> panel regression new cases -> the reproduction rates 10 days after schools closed down were lower than the moment the schools were closed.

Types of countries are more at risk in pandamic

-> The reported number of cases in low- income countries are not much accurate
-> Some other features are playing the main role but we see only their impact through these strange features.

Do we really save lives by staying at home

-> Prophet Model: originally introduced to track daily, weekly, and yearly trends, in presence of changing points and special events. Explainable parameter and easier to tune than ARIMA. Fast, easy to implement, and use.

Summary

In terms of completion, we were actually doing well. So from a technical perspective, I learned a lot. Although some questions are good, the methods are problematic. But for time-related data, time-related questions are always good to ask, like how many days after the government controlling will affect the daily new cases? I also learnt some tools for analysis,like Prophet.
